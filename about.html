Causes of Air Pollution

Air pollution results from various sources, including vehicle emissions, industrial discharges, agricultural activities, and natural events like wildfires. Pollutants, such as carbon dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter, accumulate in the atmosphere, degrading air quality and causing health risks.

Effects of Air Pollution

Air pollution has severe consequences for public health, leading to respiratory diseases, cardiovascular issues, and premature death. It also damages ecosystems, acidifies soils and water bodies, and contributes to the loss of biodiversity. Addressing air pollution benefits both people and the planet, creating a healthier, more sustainable environment.
